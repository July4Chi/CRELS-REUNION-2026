### Basic Experiment: Pine Method (Safest)

**Objective:** Test if pine materials can measurably alter stone hardness

**Materials:**

- Fresh pine needles (200-500g)
- Pine resin/sap (50-100g)
- Large pinecone (Fibonacci 8:13 or 13:21 spiral)
- Small limestone sample (10cm cube)
- 528Hz sound source (tuning fork or tone generator)
- Mohs hardness test kit

**Procedure:**

1. **Baseline measurement:**
- Test limestone hardness (should not scratch with copper penny)
1. **Pine extract preparation:**
- Grind pine needles with water
- Strain to extract liquid
- Add small amount of pine resin (heat gently to dissolve)
1. **Application:**
- Apply pine liquid to stone surface
- Let soak 2-4 hours
- Coat with thin layer of pine resin
1. **Acoustic activation:**
- Place pinecone on stone surface
- Hold 528Hz tuning fork against pinecone
- Continue for 30-60 minutes
1. **Test for softening:**
- Can copper penny scratch it now? (Mohs ~3.5 test)
- Is surface tacky/pliable?
- Document changes
1. **Observe re-hardening:**
- Wait 30 minutes after acoustic phase
- Re-test hardness
- Check if returns to baseline

**Expected Results:**

- **Null:** No change (theory disproven or parameters incorrect)
- **Moderate:** Surface becomes slightly softer (partial success)
- **Strong:** Measurable hardness reduction (supports theory)
